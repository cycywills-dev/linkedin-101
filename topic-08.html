Scams & fake profiles to watch for

👤 Too-good-to-be-true offers
Unsolicited DMs offering high salaries for vague roles with no interview are almost always scams.
💸 Requests for money / gift cards
Legitimate recruiters never ask you to pay fees, buy equipment, or send payment.
🔗 Suspicious links in messages
Never click unknown links in DMs — even from connections. Hover before clicking.
🧑‍💻 Fake recruiter profiles
Few connections, stock photo, no history, recent account — verify on the company site.
💬 Romance / trust scams
Scammers build rapport over weeks before a financial ask. Be wary of overly personal DMs.
🛡️ When in doubt, slow down
Urgency is a tactic. Verify independently before sharing anything or acting.

Stay safe online

Protect your account

🔐 Enable 2FA
Settings → Sign in & Security → Two-step verification. Use an authenticator app over SMS.
🔑 Strong, unique password
Never reuse your email password. Use a manager like Bitwarden or 1Password.
📧 Watch for phishing
LinkedIn never asks for your password by email. Log in via the official site.
👁️ Review active sessions
Settings → Security → Where you're signed in. Remove devices you don't recognise.
🔒 Control visibility
Settings → Visibility — choose who sees your connections, email, and activity.
✅ Two minutes, today
Most of these take under five minutes — set them up before anything else.

Why profiles get restricted

Stay within LinkedIn's User Agreement — these behaviours trigger restrictions or bans.

🤖 Bot-like behaviour
Mass connection requests sent too fast trip spam filters.
📛 Fake name / impersonation
False names or fictional profiles violate the User Agreement.
📢 Spam / aggressive promo
Repeated unsolicited sales messages or duplicated content.
🚫 Prohibited content
Hate speech, harassment, misinformation, or adult content.
📋 Multiple accounts
One person = one profile. Extra personal profiles break policy.
🔔 Too many rejections
Many “I don't know this person” marks restrict your connecting.
